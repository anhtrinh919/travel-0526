Here are the top-rated and most-reviewed eating spots and cafes for your trip. These are curated based on high Google Maps review counts (1,000+) and high ratings (4.5+), focusing on authentic flavors suitable for a family.

May 16: Tuy Hoa Arrival & City Evening
Since you arrive at 1:30 PM, these spots are ideal for a late lunch or snack and a landmark dinner.

Lunch/Late Snack:

Bánh Mì Chấm Nguyễn Huệ (94 Nguyễn Huệ): A quintessential local snack. Dip crispy bread into a flavorful bowl of meatballs and egg. (Fast, local).

Bánh Canh Hẹ Thành Tùng (Lê Lợi): A must-try Phu Yen specialty covered in chives.

Dinner:

Quán Bà Tám (289 Lê Duẩn): The #1 spot for Ocean Tuna Eye. It’s nutritious and unique. (2,000+ reviews).

Bếp Cố Đô (417 Hùng Vương): Higher-end but very "local" soul; excellent for a family dinner with diverse Central dishes.

Cafe:

Huy Tùng Coffee (125 Nguyễn Trãi): A heritage coffee brand in Phu Yen. Traditional, shaded, and very "Phu Yen."

Noon Concept (An Dương Vương): Modern, minimalist, and very popular for photos.

May 17: South Route (Vũng Rô / Mũi Điện)
Lunch (On the Water):

Bè nổi Vi Anh or Bè nổi Thanh Niên (Vũng Rô Bay): These are floating rafts. You pick live seafood from the cages. (Most popular raft spots).

Dinner (Back in City):

Cơm Niêu Năm Ánh (394 Hùng Vương): Family-style claypot rice with hundreds of reviews. Perfect for kids. (2,500+ reviews).

Nem Nướng Nhật Hoàng (04 Trần Bình Trọng): Grilled pork rolls; interactive and fun for kids to roll themselves.

Cafe:

Koi Cafe (An Dương Vương): Huge space with a large fish pond—kids love it while parents chill.

May 18: North Route (Đầm Ô Loan / Gành Đá Đĩa)
Lunch (Lagoon Seafood):

Quán Tuấn or Nhà hàng Thúy Kiều (Cầu An Hải): Located right on the lagoon. Order the Blood Cockles (sò huyết) and steamed grouper.

Dinner (Back in City):

Bún cá Đất Phú (169 Lê Thánh Tôn): Clear, light fish noodle soup. Very popular with locals for a clean dinner.

Cháo Hàu 373 (373 Nguyễn Huệ): Fresh oyster porridge—creamy and soothing for the evening.

Cafe:

Alice - Tea Room (An Dương Vương): A beautiful garden villa cafe with a vintage vibe.

May 19: The Migration (Transfer to Quy Nhon)
Lunch (Stopover):

Quán Hải Sản Gành Đỏ (Sông Cầu): Located at the halfway point. Sông Cầu is the lobster capital of Vietnam.

Dinner (Quy Nhon City):

Bếp Nhà Xứ Nẫu (68 Nam Cao): Highly Recommended. It feels like dining in a traditional Vietnamese garden home. High quality, non-touristy vibe. (1,000+ reviews, 4.7 stars).

Lẩu Cua Ông Minh (29 Diên Hồng): Famous crab hotpot; the broth is rich and naturally sweet.

Cafe:

Surf Bar (Quy Nhon Beach): The most iconic beach cafe. Go at sunset for the best views.

May 20: Island Day (Hòn Khô / Nhơn Hải)
Lunch (Village Seafood):

Hương Dương Seafood (Nhơn Hải Village): The highest-rated seafood spot in the fishing village. Very fresh and reasonably priced.

Dinner (Quy Nhon City):

Bún cá Ngọc Liên (379 Nguyễn Huệ): Massive menu of fish and jellyfish noodles. (4,000+ reviews).

Gia Vỹ 2 (14 Diên Hồng): The best Bánh Xèo Tôm Nhảy (Jumping Shrimp Pancakes). (3,000+ reviews).

Cafe:

Adiuvat Coffee Roasters (Nguyễn Huệ): For serious coffee lovers. Specialty beans and a quiet, focused atmosphere.

May 21: Coast Route (Trung Lương / Cát Tiến)
Lunch (Cát Tiến Beach):

Nhà Hàng Cánh Quạt: Located near the giant wind turbines. Good seafood and airy views.

Dinner (Street Food Tour):

Ngô Văn Sở Food Street: A paradise for street food. Try the grilled skewers, Tré, and fruit smoothies.

Chè Nhớ (134 Ngô Mây): The most famous dessert shop in Quy Nhon (established since 1990).

Cafe:

Marina Coffee (05 Đô Đốc Bảo): A grand, high-ceiling cafe with views over the city square. Good for a final "chill" session.

Important Note for Mid-May:
Opening Times: Many local spots like Cơm gà Tuyết Nhung or Bánh mì Chấm close by 2:00 PM and reopen in the late afternoon. Plan to eat lunch early (around 11:30 AM) to avoid the heat and the midday closure.

Pro-Tip for "Self-Touring" with a Child
Hon Kho / Hon Nua: Instead of booking a 7:00 AM tour from the city, ride there yourself at 9:00 AM. You’ll miss the big tour groups, the water will be clearer under the high sun, and you can negotiate a private canoe for just your family for around 600k–800k total (excluding lunch).

Safety: Always ask for a child-sized life jacket (áo phao trẻ em) before the boat leaves the dock. Most local boats have them, but you have to ask specifically.
